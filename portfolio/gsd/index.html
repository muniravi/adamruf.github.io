Email

Tools and resources to help you tackle the neverending challenge of managing email.

  • Inbox Zero

    http://inboxzero.com/

    Maybe you've heard of Inbox Zero, now you can fully wrap your head around this philosophy of keeping your brain out of your inbox.

  • Boomerang for Gmail

    http://www.boomeranggmail.com/

    It's the feature that the Google team forgot to implement! Boomerang for Gmail empowers you to schedule email to be sent later or set email reminders easily.

  • Auto Text Expander for Google Chrome

    https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/auto-text-expander-for-go/iibninhmiggehlcdolcilmhacighjamp

    Do you feel like you're constantly emailing the same phrases 10 times a day? Great for saving time writing emails, Auto Text Expander allows you to set abbreviations that will automagically expand into the attached phrase.

  • The Email Game

    http://emailga.me/

    From the folks who brought you the amazing Boomerang for Gmail, here's the email management game!

  • Jing ( )

    http://www.techsmith.com/jing.html

    Have you ever felt that it would be easier or faster to send a screencast than to type up an email? Jing is a free screen recording tool that constrains you to creating a video of 5 minutes or less. Tip: Engineers love receiving screencasts of the steps to reproduce the bug you just found.

Habits

Creating new habits and sticking to them isn't easy, but these resources will help you turn desired behaviors into hard-to-break habits.

  • Stickk

    http://www.stickk.com/

    Stickk is a psychologist-approved way to set and achieve goals, like developing new habits. It makes effective use of two powerful motivators: peers and fear of loss. The silver bullet is the option to donate your hard-earned cash to an ant-charity if you fail to meet your goal.

  • Lift

    https://lift.do/

    Lift is another online service aimed at helping you build and maintain new habits.
