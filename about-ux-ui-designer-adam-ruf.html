About Adam

It's become increasingly clear that investing in user experience design is no longer just a competitive advantage. Ease-of-use and pleasure are quickly joining consumers' basic expectations of functionality and reliability.

Bio

Adam Ruf is a veteran user experience designer who, for more than 6 years, has worked with agile software development teams like BrightRoll (current) and UserTesting.com. While he's worn a variety of hats, Adam's primary responsibilities have included understanding usersâ€™ needs and goals, discovering problems, rapidly iterating through designs, delivering meaningful features to users while improving their overall experience, and moving the needle for business metrics that matter.

Since 2008, Adam has supported entrepreneurs, startups, and non-profits through his website, adamruf.com/adamruf.com, where he provides freelance UX/UI design and web development services.

A contributor to the UXD community, Adam has taught both intermediate user experience design and intermediate web design at the Academy of Art University in San Francisco. He maintains a list of resources for UX and UI designers on his website and, in 2014, he also designed an industry survey for UX Professionals.

When he's not working, Adam enjoys building his latest pet software project, attending design meetups and workshops in SF, traveling locally and globally, hiking, photography, watching stand-up comedy, hosting board game parties, and collecting art toys.
