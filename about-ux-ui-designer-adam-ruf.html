About Adam

It's become increasingly clear that investing in user experience design is no longer just a competitive advantage. Ease-of-use and pleasure are quickly joining consumers' basic expectations of functionality and reliability.

Bio

Adam Ruf is a senior user experience designer who has worked with agile product and engineering teams like BrightRoll (Yahoo) and UserTesting for more than seven years. As a UX designer, his primary responsibilities have focused on understanding users’ needs and goals, discovering problems, and rapidly iterating to deliver useful, usable features and enjoyable experiences.

Since 2008, Adam has supported entrepreneurs, startups, and non-profits through his website, adamruf.com, where he provides freelance UX design and web development services.

A contributor to the community, Adam taught user experience design and web design at the Academy of Art University in San Francisco and mentored junior UX designers through Designlab and CascadeSF's UXNight. His curated list of resources for UX designers continues to help the next generation of user experience designers.

When he's not working, Adam enjoys hobby projects, attending design events, learning new languages, traveling, hiking, hosting board game parties, and collecting art toys.
